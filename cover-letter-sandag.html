My most directly relevant experience is a year as a GIS and Economic Development Intern for Humboldt County, where I built geospatial pipelines in Python and ArcGIS Pro to map regional assets, identify infrastructure gaps, and produce planning deliverables used in county executive briefings. That work taught me how to translate spatial analysis into outputs that non-technical decision-makers can actually act on, which I understand is a core part of what your team does.

Outside of professional work, I have spent the past year building a portfolio of Python-developed GIS projects that I think align closely with SANDAG's focus areas. One project analyzed transit-oriented development along Sound Transit's Link expansion, joining 531,000 King County parcel records to light rail stations, engineering distance-band features, and running a regression to quantify the land value premium within walkable distance of stations. Another built an automated pipeline to cluster pedestrian and bike collision records, score high-injury locations by a composite risk index, and produce a prioritized list for infrastructure investment, directly relevant to active transportation planning. For both projects I built interactive web maps in Python and MapLibre GL to present findings in a format accessible beyond a static report. The full portfolio, including a live scrollytelling page with the interactive maps, is at mossfunki.github.io.
