Transit-Oriented Development & Affordability Model

Where are transit's benefits priced out of reach? OLS regression across 531k King County parcels quantifies the price premium of transit proximity, controlling for spatial autocorrelation via lag terms. Spatial joins link parcel records to GTFS stop distances, walk-score buffers, and zoning overlays. The output maps where affordability gaps widen most relative to access — actionable for where to target upzoning, density bonuses, or below-market unit requirements.

Transportation Energy · Network Optimization

Hydrogen Corridor Routing & Infrastructure Gap Analysis

Which route minimizes fuel — and where do coverage gaps in the refueling network expose that route to range risk? Energy-weighted Dijkstra on the real OSM road network (SRTM terrain, NREL station data) finds the flat I-880 corridor uses 18.8% less H₂ than the shorter Niles Canyon route. Infrastructure gap analysis with spatial constraints (grade ≤ 5%, spacing ≥ 10 mi) identifies where new stations close coverage without over-building.

Demand Forecasting & Safety Stock Optimization

How much inventory do you hold, and when do you reorder? Prophet time-series model captures event-driven demand seasonality and promotional spikes; safety stock thresholds recalculate dynamically as forecasts shift rather than sitting at a static reorder point. 31% fewer stockouts vs. a fixed-threshold baseline on held-out test data — a direct reduction in lost-sale cost without proportional increases in carrying cost.

Explore the Data

Capital, housing value, wages, and climate exposure all concentrate in geographic patterns that shape where people and businesses invest. Four national-scale analyses — each asking where a macroeconomic or environmental outcome clusters in space. Scroll to navigate.

↓
01 / 04

Financial Flows

Which cities attract capital — and which export it? Directional arcs map intermetro flows between major US financial hubs; arc weight reflects transaction volume. The network structure reveals which cities are capital sinks and which depend on external financial supply.

02 / 04

Real Estate Markets

Where are housing markets heating up — and do appreciating markets cluster spatially? County-level YoY appreciation rates reveal regional co-movement: Sun Belt markets moving together, coastal markets diverging from inland. Heat intensity reflects rate of change, not price level.

03 / 04

Labor Markets

Where does high-wage work concentrate — and does it track with employment scale? Circle size reflects metro employment; color encodes median wages. The pattern shows the geographic concentration of economic opportunity and the gap between coastal metros and the interior.

04 / 04

Climate Risk Index

Which counties face compounding climate risk — and does vulnerability cluster geographically? K-Means on FEMA NRI composite scores (hazard × exposure × resilience) produces five risk tiers at county resolution, averaged to the state level to surface regional patterns that county-level noise obscures.
