Demand Forecasting & Inventory Optimization

Problem: Static reorder models miss event-driven demand spikes — generating stockouts during Seahawks games or PAX and excess inventory in quiet weeks.

Method: Prophet time-series forecasting trained on 2 years of simulated weekly demand. Safety stock recalculated weekly using service level targets (95%) and lead time variance. Dynamic reorder point replaces fixed tables.

Output: Streamlit dashboard with 14-day rolling forecast per SKU and a weekly reorder action table — built for an ops manager to act on without opening a notebook.

31% fewer stockouts vs. static model 8.4% MAPE (held-out test) Dynamic ROP per SKU

User Retention Analysis — 60% Lift

Problem: An early-stage AI startup was losing users with no visibility into where or why. No analytics function, no benchmarks, no competitive context.

Method: Behavioral cohort analysis in Amplitude to identify drop-off by segment and action. Weekly market intelligence feed from 20+ sources to frame competitive positioning. SQL + Python to pull and structure the underlying data.

Output: Weekly exec briefs with clear recommendations. Product team adopted the findings — retention increased 60% within 8 weeks of implementation.

Seattle Last-Mile Delivery Gap Analysis

Problem: Where are the King County zones that existing Seattle distribution hubs can't reach within a viable delivery window — and how bad is the gap?

Method: OSMnx network analysis generates 10/20/30-minute drive-time isochrones from five real distribution hub locations. Census tract overlay produces a composite under-service score by zone.

Output: Interactive Leaflet map with gap-scored zones. Identifies candidate hub locations for a new facility to maximize under-served coverage.
