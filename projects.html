Austin Multifamily Investment Scorer

A census-tract–level scoring model that highlights where multifamily investment is most promising in Austin, TX.

Project overview

This project builds a quantitative investment score for each census tract in Austin. The score blends demographics, rental demand, and accessibility into a single 0–100 metric to help investors and planners compare locations quickly.

Key inputs

  • Median income and rent levels from Census / ACS data.
  • Renter share as a proxy for multifamily demand.
  • Distance to downtown Austin and major employment centers.
  • Composite score normalized across tracts.

What I built

  • GeoPandas workflow to join demographic data with tract geometries.
  • Scoring functions to standardize variables and combine them into a single index.
  • Interactive Folium web map with hover popups and tract-level details.
  • Exploratory plots to show distributions and relationships between drivers and scores.

Takeaways

  • Tracts within roughly five miles of downtown show consistently higher scores, reflecting strong demand and accessibility.
  • Renter share and proximity to job centers are strong drivers of multifamily potential, but high scores also depend on income levels that can support new supply.
  • The scoring framework is portable—by swapping in local data, it can be adapted to other metros or asset classes.
